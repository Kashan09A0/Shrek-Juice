Juice Process

Cleaning and Washing

Thoroughly clean the fruits or vegetables to remove dirt, pesticides, and any contaminants. This step is crucial to maintain hygiene and safety standards..

Peeling and Cutting

Depending on the type of produce, peeling and cutting might be necessary. For instance, citrus fruits may need peeling, while others might require chopping into smaller pieces for efficient juicing.

Juicing Process

Use juicers or extractors to obtain the juice. There are different types of juicers, such as centrifugal, masticating, or hydraulic presses, each suited for different fruits and vegetables.

Filtration

After extraction, the juice might undergo filtration to remove pulp, seeds, or any remaining solids, resulting in a smoother and clearer juice.

Pasteurization

Some manufacturers pasteurize juice by briefly heating it to kill bacteria and extend its shelf life. However, this step can affect the taste and nutritional content.

Packaging

Fill the juice into appropriate containers, such as bottles, cartons, or pouches, ensuring proper sealing and labeling.
